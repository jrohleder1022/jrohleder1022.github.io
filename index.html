Theory Reference

The formulas behind every number on this dashboard

Statistics

Portfolio Variance

σ²ₚ = wᵀ Σ w

Where w = weights vector, Σ = covariance matrix. When correlations ρ between assets are below 1, total variance is less than the weighted sum of individual variances. This is the mathematical basis of diversification — the only free lunch in finance.

Optimization

Sharpe Ratio

S = (μₚ − r_f) / σₚ

Return per unit of risk. Maximize this by taking ∂S/∂wᵢ = 0 for all i. The tangency portfolio (highest Sharpe) is where the capital market line touches the efficient frontier. Concentrated portfolios usually have lower Sharpe than diversified ones at the same return level.

Position Sizing

Kelly Criterion

f* = (bp − q) / b

Optimal bet size given edge. p = win probability, q = 1−p, b = win/loss ratio. Most pros use "half-Kelly" because full Kelly is too volatile. If you assume 60% probability NVDA outperforms with 1.5:1 payoff, full Kelly says ~33%. Half-Kelly: ~17%. You currently hold 36% — implying you have more edge than half-Kelly assumes.

Asset Pricing

Fama-French 5-Factor

R = α + β_M·MKT + β_S·SMB + β_V·HML + β_P·RMW + β_I·CMA + ε

Explains ~95% of variation in stock returns via Market, Size, Value, Profitability, Investment factors. Your portfolio loads heavily on MKT and inversely on HML (anti-value, i.e. growth). The α (alpha) is what's left — true skill or luck. Hard to distinguish without 20+ years of data.

Concentration

HHI & N_effective

HHI = Σwᵢ² × 10,000 N_eff = 1/Σwᵢ²

HHI: antitrust measure repurposed for portfolios. <1500 = diversified. 1500-2500 = moderate. >2500 = highly concentrated. N_eff converts your HHI into "equivalent equal-weighted holdings." Your 4 stocks act like ~3.

Lifecycle

Merton's Solution

w* = (μ − r_f) / (γ · σ²)

Optimal equity allocation given relative risk aversion γ. For young investors, human capital acts as a bond — so the optimal financial portfolio tilts MORE toward equity to balance overall wealth. Justifies aggression at 22. Does NOT specifically justify single-stock concentration — that's a separate decision requiring genuine edge.
